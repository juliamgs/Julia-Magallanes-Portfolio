SkyMood: In-Flight Anxiety Management

Transforming air travel through personalized anxiety relief

Role

UX Designer & Researcher

Timeline

Aug 2024 - Dec 2024

Tools

Figma, MiroBoard

Team

Team Bread (4 members)

Problem Statement

Flight anxiety, particularly concerning turbulence and feelings of unease, is a significant challenge in air travel, affecting a considerable number of passengers.

Current inflight systems primarily aim to distract rather than directly address the psychological concerns of passengers. Pilots rarely provide updates during turbulence, and flight attendants have limited ability to offer personalized reassurance.

Solution

SkyMood is a transformative in-flight anxiety management system that personalizes entertainment content based on pre-flight anxiety assessments, offering passengers transparency during turbulence.

The system consists of:

  • Mobile App: Pre-flight anxiety assessment and personalized recommendations
  • In-Flight Entertainment: AI-curated content based on passenger needs
  • Real-Time Insights: Flight mechanics education and turbulence transparency
  • Code Transfer System: Seamless integration between mobile and in-flight screens

Design Flow

1. Onboarding and Survey Completion

Users begin by opening the SkyMood mobile app before their flight. A personal survey helps capture individual preferences, flight anxiety levels, frequency of flying, and desired coping mechanisms.

2. Code Transfer for Integration

Upon survey completion, the app generates a unique code. Users input this code into the aircraft's in-flight entertainment screen upon boarding to connect their personal data.

3. Personalized Infotainment Experience

The in-flight system uses AI-generated recommendations to curate:

  • Relaxing or calming games and videos
  • Real-time weather and flight status updates
  • Education about flight mechanics (e.g., turbulence explanations)
  • Dashboard layout organizing features for quick access

Key Design Decisions

Personal Survey Integration

What makes this design decision exciting is how it creates a personalized experience through an elegantly simple but powerful onboarding process. By addressing anxiety and flight-related stressors, the design creates a user-centered approach to in-flight entertainment—moving from distraction to proactive support.

The survey collects data such as:

  • User's Flight Mood
  • Top Flight Concerns
  • Entertainment Interests

Personalized AI Infotainment Screen

A standout feature is the personalized AI infotainment screen after the passenger takes the personal survey in the app. All of the user's information is generated using AI. The specific types of games and activities presented to the user are based on the information they gave during the survey.

This AI system calculates the best activities for the user's anxieties, stressors, or distractions that would best accommodate them during their flight.

Design Process

Tools & Methodologies

The project was developed using Figma for prototyping and MiroBoard for ideation and journey mapping. Early sketches and journey mapping were created on MiroBoard for easier group collaboration, while Figma's flexibility allowed us to iterate quickly based on user feedback.

Design Evolution

The design evolved significantly over time through multiple stages:

Early Sketches and Paper Prototypes

Initial iterations focused on a broad array of features, including turbulence statistics, weather updates, and general entertainment options. Feedback indicated these designs lacked focus and overwhelmed users with too much information.

Wireframes

We emphasized user flows and hierarchy, refining the onboarding survey to focus on key anxiety metrics. This was informed by user evaluations which highlighted the importance of a straightforward onboarding experience.

Initial Digital Prototype

Integrating feedback, the digital prototype introduced a simplified survey flow and more cohesive dashboard layout. However, additional iterations were needed to optimize personalization features.

Final Digital Prototype

The final design incorporated major updates, such as an interactive dashboard for flight insights and a streamlined code transfer process, offering a personalized, anxiety-relief-focused experience.

Major Design Changes

Simplified Survey Flow

Early iterations presented a long, detailed survey that overwhelmed users. User feedback led to the implementation of bite-sized, easy-to-navigate steps, improving the onboarding experience and encouraging survey completion.

Before vs. After

The original survey had 15+ questions on multiple screens with complex navigation. The redesigned survey features 5-7 key questions with clear visual progress indicators and simple selection options.

Addition of Flight Insights Dashboard

Initial designs lacked real-time flight mechanics information, which was a key concern for users. Feedback from user evaluations and instructors emphasized the need for reassurance through transparency. Adding this feature directly addressed these needs, providing passengers with real-time updates and educational content about turbulence.

User Research & Testing

Research Methods

We employed diverse user research methods to gain comprehensive insights:

  • Surveys: Quantitative data on anxiety triggers and preferences
  • Interviews: In-depth understanding of passenger experiences
  • Usability Testing: Iterative testing of prototypes
  • Journey Mapping: Visualizing the end-to-end passenger experience

Key Findings

Passengers want transparency, not just distraction. While entertainment helps, users expressed a strong desire to understand what's happening during turbulence and why certain sensations occur.

Personalization is critical. What calms one passenger may increase anxiety in another, making personalized content recommendations essential.

Detailed Design Features

Anxiety Assessment Categories

The survey collects three types of critical data:

  • Flight Mood: Current anxiety level and emotional state
  • Top Flight Concerns: Specific triggers (turbulence, takeoff/landing, confined spaces)
  • Entertainment Interests: Preferred activities and content types

AI Content Curation

The system uses machine learning to match content to passenger needs:

  • Calming games for high-anxiety passengers
  • Educational content about flight mechanics for knowledge-seekers
  • Entertainment options tailored to personal preferences
  • Real-time weather and flight status for transparency

Privacy & Data Handling

Given the sensitive nature of anxiety data, we designed with privacy as a priority:

  • Data is stored locally and deleted after the flight
  • Code system ensures no personal information is transmitted
  • Clear privacy policy and data usage explanations
  • User control over data sharing preferences

Design Theory & Best Practices

Our design process aligned with established UX principles:

User Flows & Task Analysis

We created comprehensive user flows to visualize the passenger journey from pre-flight to post-flight. This approach, emphasized in Interaction Design: Beyond Human-Computer Interaction, helped us identify pain points and optimize interactions.

Iterative Design

Multiple rounds of prototyping and testing allowed us to refine the interface based on real user feedback, ensuring the final design truly met passenger needs.

Multi-Modal Interface Design

The seamless integration between mobile and in-flight screens demonstrates best practices in cross-platform UX design, maintaining consistency while optimizing for each device's unique context.

Impact

SkyMood has the potential to redefine the in-flight experience for passengers who experience anxiety:

  • Proactive Support: Unlike traditional systems that only distract, SkyMood addresses psychological needs directly
  • Passenger Empowerment: Provides transparency and understanding, reducing feelings of helplessness
  • Airline Differentiation: Positions airlines as innovative and passenger-centric
  • Operational Benefits: Reduces anxiety-related incidents, improving flight operations

Potential Business Impact

By offering a personalized and anxiety-aware experience, airlines can improve passenger satisfaction and loyalty, creating a competitive advantage in a crowded market. Fewer anxiety-related incidents could also result in smoother operations for flight attendants.

Challenges & Considerations

Data Security & Privacy

Collecting sensitive information about passengers' anxiety levels and emotional states requires robust security measures. Our design ensures passengers have full transparency and control over how their data is utilized to maintain trust.

Implementation Constraints

Despite the tools' capabilities, user testing was limited by time and resources. Testing primarily involved simulated scenarios, which may not fully capture the real-world stress of air travel and its impact on user needs.

Ethical Considerations

We carefully considered the ethical implications of collecting and using anxiety data, ensuring our design respects passenger vulnerability and provides genuine support rather than manipulation.

Next Steps

Future enhancements for SkyMood could include:

  • Partnership with airlines for pilot programs
  • Integration with wearable devices for biometric feedback
  • Expanded content library with diverse calming activities
  • Multi-language support for international passengers
  • Post-flight analytics to measure effectiveness and improve recommendations
