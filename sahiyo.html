Sahiyo: Global Network Map Redesign

Amplifying survivor voices to end Female Genital Cutting

Role

Remote UX Designer & Researcher

Timeline

Sep 2024 - Apr 2025

Tools

Figma, User Research

Team

4 UX Designers

About Sahiyo

Sahiyo is a nonprofit organization on a mission to empower Asian and other communities to end Female Genital Cutting (FGC), using dialogue, education, and collaborative efforts to create positive social change.

The organization has three core goals:

  • End the practice of female genital cutting, a UNICEF and UNFPA-recognized human rights violation
  • Enable a culture where a woman/girl's body and female sexuality is not feared or suppressed
  • Recognize and emphasize the values of consent and a child/woman's right to their own body

Problem Statement

Sahiyo's "Voices to End FGM/C" interactive Global Network Map is overly text-heavy and lacks visual appeal, making navigation difficult. This limits user engagement and prevents individuals from fully exploring the stories of FGM/C survivors.

As a result, awareness and support for Sahiyo's mission are diminished, undermining the map's potential as an effective advocacy tool.

Solution

Our goal is to redesign Sahiyo's interactive Global Network Map to make it more visually appealing and functional, effectively raising greater awareness about female genital mutilation/cutting (FGM/C).

The redesign focuses on:

  • Increasing visibility of survivor stories through improved visual hierarchy
  • Enhancing navigation and user engagement
  • Creating an accessible experience across devices
  • Better communicating with stakeholders including funders and conference attendees

Target Users & Stakeholders

Primary Users

Individuals with limited or no prior knowledge of FGM/C who want to learn more about the issue and survivor experiences.

Key Stakeholders

  • Communications Director: Manages the website and content
  • Project Managers: Our direct contacts and project guides
  • Potential Funders: Organizations evaluating Sahiyo's impact
  • Conferences & Boards: Advocacy platforms showcasing Sahiyo's work
  • Third-Party Developer: Will implement our design specifications

Research & Discovery

User Research Methods

We conducted comprehensive user research to identify usability barriers and understand user needs:

  • User Interviews: Qualitative insights into user needs and pain points
  • Surveys: Quantitative data to validate design decisions
  • Competitive Analysis: Understanding best practices from similar advocacy platforms
  • User Personas: Creating empathy tools for the design process

Key Findings

Users struggled with the text-heavy interface and found it difficult to discover and engage with survivor stories. The lack of visual hierarchy and intuitive navigation prevented users from fully understanding Sahiyo's impact.

Design Process

Low-Fidelity Wireframing

We began with low-fidelity wireframes to explore different layout options and information architectures, focusing on improving visual hierarchy and story discoverability.

High-Fidelity Prototyping

After iterating on wireframes based on user feedback, we created high-fidelity, interactive prototypes that transformed the text-heavy interface into a visually engaging, accessible experience across devices.

Key Design Decisions

Visual Hierarchy & Story Prominence

We redesigned the map interface to give survivor stories visual prominence through:

  • Large, engaging preview cards with imagery
  • Clear categorization by region and theme
  • Interactive map markers that draw attention to story locations
  • Reduced text density with strategic use of white space

Sensitive Content Handling

Given the sensitive nature of FGM/C survivor stories, we carefully balanced:

  • Privacy Considerations: Protecting survivor identities while sharing impactful stories
  • Content Warnings: Clear indicators for potentially triggering content
  • Ethical Guidelines: Collaborating with international team to ensure appropriate presentation
  • Respectful Tone: Design elements that honor survivor experiences

Cross-Device Accessibility

The redesign ensures the map is fully accessible across devices, from mobile phones to desktop displays, allowing users to engage with content anywhere.

Usability Testing & Iteration

We performed iterative usability testing to inform design decisions and validate our approach:

  • Conducted multiple rounds of user testing with target audience
  • Analyzed feedback to identify patterns and trends
  • Made design iterations based on test results
  • Validated improvements through follow-up testing

Testing Goals

Our goal is to achieve a 20% increase in user engagement metrics including click-through rate, time spent on the page, and number of survivor stories viewed.

Collaboration & Project Handoff

Throughout the project, we collaborated closely with Sahiyo's international team and nonprofit stakeholders to ensure the design met organizational needs and ethical standards.

UX Specification Documentation

We developed comprehensive UX specification and handoff documentation for third-party developer implementation, including:

  • Detailed wireframes and prototypes
  • Interaction specifications
  • Content guidelines and requirements
  • Accessibility standards
  • Future roadmap and feature recommendations

Impact & Next Steps

By solving the usability problems of the Global Network Map:

  • FGM/C survivor stories will be more visible and accessible to global audiences
  • Sahiyo can more effectively convey their impact to potential funders and partners
  • Key stakeholders will be better informed and inclined to contribute to an FGM/C-free world
  • User engagement is expected to increase by 20% through improved usability

Social Impact

This redesign represents more than just improved UX—it's about amplifying voices that deserve to be heard and advancing the global movement to end FGM/C.

Future Enhancements

Potential future features identified through our research:

  • Multi-language support for global accessibility
  • Advanced filtering by story themes and regions
  • Community discussion forums
  • Resource libraries for educators and activists
